## Identity

**Name:** Orbit
**Tagline:** Vibe-commerce rails for the next generation of global merchants
**Role / Title:** Open infrastructure marketplace for commerce operations
**Industry / Field:** B2B SaaS / Commerce Infrastructure / Operations-as-a-Service
**Since:** 2026 (pre-launch, early access phase)

## Audience

### Primary — Merchants

**Who:** DTC and e-commerce brands, especially cross-border operators and global brands entering the US market. Founders and brand leads aged 25-45 who are creatively driven but operationally overwhelmed.
**Mindset:** They started a brand because they had taste, vision, product instinct. They did not start a brand to manage compliance brokers, warehouse operators, and logistics coordinators. They want to stay in their "vibe zone" — brand, product, creative — and hand everything else to infrastructure they trust.
**What they come for:** Relief. A clear path to offloading 80% of operational burden without hiring a COO or stitching together 15 freelancers.

### Primary — Specialists

**Who:** Compliance brokers, performance marketers, logistics coordinators, warehouse operators, e-commerce managers, operations coordinators, market researchers. Independent operators or small firms aged 28-50 who are excellent at their function but lack merchant pipeline and tech infrastructure.
**Mindset:** They are tired of cold outreach, building their own tools, and losing clients to agencies. They want a plug-in model: bring expertise, get matched to merchants, let AI handle admin.
**What they come for:** Merchant access, earnings potential, AI-assisted workflow, and infrastructure they do not have to build.

### Secondary

**Who:** Investors, press, potential partners, API integrators, commerce ecosystem builders.
**What they come for:** Validation that this model is real, differentiated, and inevitable.

## Emotional Direction

**Should feel:** Inevitable, intelligent, calm confidence
**Should NEVER feel:** Corporate SaaS, cluttered, feature-list heavy
**Emotional brief:** "The site should feel like stepping into a mission control room for the future of commerce — quiet authority, deep intelligence, everything in its place — not like scrolling through another SaaS landing page with blue gradients and screenshot carousels."
**Brand archetype:** Sage + Ruler hybrid — Orbit knows something the rest of the market has not figured out yet (Sage) and presents it with the quiet, structured authority of infrastructure that is already inevitable (Ruler). This is not a startup pitching a dream. This is the system presenting itself.

## Visual Language & Principles

**Palette philosophy:** Dark-mode native. The name is Orbit — the visual system should feel like deep space. Not "dark theme" as an afterthought, but darkness as the primary medium. Light is used sparingly and intentionally: accent glows, data highlights, interactive states.

**Grid & layout philosophy:** Structured like mission control. Generous whitespace (or rather, "dark space"). Grid-based layouts that feel engineered, not decorated. Asymmetric where it serves hierarchy, rigid where it serves clarity.

**Motion principles:**
- Everything moves like orbital mechanics — deliberate, continuous, never rushed
- No bounce, no snap, no playful easing
- Smooth, gravitational transitions: elements drift into place
- Scroll-driven animations tied to content progression
- The orbital diagram should feel alive — perpetual subtle motion even at rest
- Parallax and depth to reinforce the space metaphor without being literal

**Illustration / visual style:**
- Abstract, geometric, architectural
- Thin lines, node-and-edge diagrams, orbital paths
- Glow and luminance as the primary way to draw attention (not color fills)
- Never use generic SaaS illustration style (the "Humaaans" / undraw look)
- Never use dashboard screenshots as hero visuals

**Sections required (in order):**

### 1. Navigation
Minimal, dark, fixed. Logo (ORBIT wordmark) left. Three links center or right: For Merchants / For Specialists / Login. Primary CTA button: "Get Early Access". Nav should be nearly transparent on hero, solidify on scroll.

### 2. Hero
The opening statement. Full-bleed dark canvas.
- Headline options: "Stop running ops. Start vibing." or "Your brand deserves your full attention." (Design Director + copy to finalize)

- CTA: "Get Early Access" (email capture or scroll to form)
- Background: Animated orbital visualization — the 7 layers in subtle, continuous motion. Not decorative — this IS the product concept made visual.

### 3. The Problem
Two-column comparison. Traditional Model vs Orbit Model.

**Traditional Model (left / "before"):**
- Scattered freelancers, no coordination
- High management overhead
- Inconsistent quality
- Expensive custom integrations
- Knowledge lost between projects
- The merchant becomes the operations manager they never wanted to be

**Orbit Model (right / "after"):**

- AI-orchestrated coordination

Visual treatment: This should feel like a system state change, not a feature comparison table. Design Director to determine whether this is literal two-column, animated transition, or progressive reveal.

### 4. The Vibe-Commerce Model
The core concept section. What merchants bring vs what Orbit brings.
- Merchant (20%): Brand vision, product direction, creative decisions, taste
- Orbit (80%): Market intelligence, compliance, operations, e-commerce management, marketing execution, logistics, warehousing
- The 80/20 split should be visualized — not just stated. This is the "aha" moment.
- Reinforce the term "vibe-commerce" — merchants stay in their vibe zone, Orbit handles the machine.

### 5. The 7 Layers — Orbital Diagram
The centerpiece interactive section. Animated orbital diagram showing all 7 functional layers orbiting a merchant at the center.

Each layer is a node on the orbital path:
1. **MIA** — Market Intelligence Agent: Monitors trends, competitors, pricing signals. Specialist: market researcher. Merchant gets: live market intelligence dashboard.
2. **CIA** — Compliance Intelligence Agent: Tracks regulatory changes, tariffs, import rules. Specialist: compliance broker. Merchant gets: compliant operations without thinking about it.
3. **OPS** — Operations Agent (central orchestrator): Connects all layers, routes tasks, manages workflows. Specialist: operations coordinator. Merchant gets: one command center.
4. **ECOM** — E-commerce Agent: Manages listings, inventory sync, platform optimization. Specialist: e-commerce manager. Merchant gets: stores that run themselves.
5. **MKT** — Marketing Agent: Content, campaigns, performance tracking. Specialist: performance marketer. Merchant gets: growth engine running in background.
6. **LOG** — Logistics Agent: Shipment tracking, carrier coordination, delivery optimization. Specialist: logistics coordinator. Merchant gets: supply chain on autopilot.
7. **WHS** — Warehousing Agent: Inventory management, fulfillment coordination. Specialist: warehouse operator. Merchant gets: physical ops handled.

**Interaction model:** Each node is clickable/hoverable to expand details. On expand, show: agent role + specialist role + what the merchant gets. The diagram must be animated — never a static table, list, or image.

**Technical note for frontend-builder:** This is the most complex component on the site. Consider Three.js for 3D orbital paths or a 2D canvas/SVG approach with GSAP for smoother performance. The OPS node should be visually differentiated as the central orchestrator. Nodes should have subtle perpetual orbital motion.

## Reference & Inspiration

**Reference sites and what to borrow:**
- **Linear.app** — The feeling of inevitability. Everything is precise, fast, dark, confident. The product feels like the future because the site feels like the future. Borrow: restraint, typographic confidence, scroll-driven storytelling.
- **Vercel.com** — Developer infrastructure that feels premium. Dark palette, technical authority, motion that serves comprehension. Borrow: how they make infrastructure feel exciting without being cluttered.
- **Stripe.com** — The gold standard for making complex infrastructure feel simple. Borrow: how they layer information progressively, never overwhelming.
- **Apple.com product pages** — Scroll-driven cinematic reveals. Borrow: the pacing, the patience, the willingness to give one idea a full viewport.

**What to avoid (traps this type of site falls into):**
- The "SaaS blue" trap: white background, blue gradient hero, card-based feature grid, dashboard screenshot. This is the default and it is death for a brand called Orbit.
- The "feature dump" trap: listing 47 features in a grid with tiny icons. Orbit has 7 layers — that is the framework. Use it as structure, not as a checklist.
- The "marketplace both-sides" trap: trying to speak to merchants and specialists equally on every section. Separate the pitches. The site is merchant-primary, specialist-secondary.
- The "startup energy" trap: exclamation marks, "we're so excited," casual tone. Orbit is calm authority. The future does not need to be excited about itself.

## Notes for Design Director

The name "Orbit" is a gift. It gives you a complete visual metaphor system: orbital paths, gravitational pull, satellites, mission control, deep space. Use it structurally, not decoratively. The 7 layers orbiting a merchant at the center is not just a diagram — it is the information architecture of the entire visual system.

The two-audience challenge is real but solvable. The site reads merchant-first. Specialists get a dedicated section (Section 6) but the overall narrative arc is: "Here is the future of commerce ops (merchant hook) -> Here is how it works (the 7 layers) -> Here is how you plug in (specialist hook) -> Get early access (both)."

The "vibe-commerce" concept is unusual and needs the site itself to embody it. If the site feels like a typical B2B SaaS page, the concept fails. The site IS the proof of concept — it should feel like the kind of thing a vibe-driven merchant would actually want to be associated with.

Typography hierarchy matters enormously on a dark site. The three-font system (Space Grotesk / DM Sans / Space Mono) gives you display, body, and data layers. Use Space Mono for the 3-letter layer codes (MIA, CIA, OPS, etc.) — they should feel like system identifiers, not marketing labels.

The 80/20 split visualization (Section 4) is the conceptual hinge of the entire site. If a visitor understands nothing else, they should understand: "I do 20% (the creative part I love), Orbit does 80% (the operational part I hate)." Design Director should make this the most memorable visual moment on the page.

Color system for the 7 layers: each layer should have a subtle color identity (within the overall blue/violet accent family) so they are distinguishable in the orbital diagram without breaking palette cohesion. Think: variations in hue and luminance, not 7 completely different colors.

## Notes for QA Reviewer

**What the client will notice immediately if wrong:**
- If the site feels like "another SaaS landing page" — generic blue, white cards, feature grids. This is the number one anxiety. The site must feel categorically different from the Stripe/Linear-inspired SaaS template ecosystem while still being as polished as those references.
- If the orbital diagram is static or looks like clip art. It must move. It must feel like a living system.
- If the copy is generic startup speak. The tone is calm authority — "the future is here, step in" not "we're disrupting commerce!"
- If merchants and specialists feel equally confused about who the site is for. Merchant-first flow, specialist-second.

**Things that would make the client say "this doesn't feel like me":**
- Light backgrounds anywhere
- Colorful SaaS illustrations
- A hero section with a dashboard mockup
- Feature comparison tables with checkmarks
- Rounded, friendly, playful aesthetic
- Any use of the word "excited" in copy

**Performance checks:**
- Orbital diagram animation must not drop below 30fps on mid-range devices
- Page load must feel fast despite Three.js/GSAP payload — critical path should render before animation libraries load
- Scroll must feel smooth (Lenis) — any jank in scroll-driven animations is a failure
- Mobile orbital diagram fallback must exist and must not be a broken desktop version

**Accessibility checks:**
- All text on dark backgrounds must meet WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for large text)
- Orbital diagram must have a non-animated accessible alternative (aria-described content or expandable list)
- Reduced-motion media query must disable all GSAP/Three.js animations gracefully
- Interactive diagram nodes must be keyboard-navigable
- CTA form must be fully accessible with proper labels and focus states
