# Oh Polly — Brand Brief

**Status:** READY_FOR_DESIGN

---

## 1. Brand Identity

**Brand name:** Oh Polly
**Tagline / positioning:** Dressed for the moment you've been waiting for.
**Origin:** United Kingdom (Glasgow, Scotland)
**Founded:** 2015
**Website target:** US brand landing page (links to existing us.ohpolly.com Shopify store — no embedded shop)

**One-line brand summary:**
Oh Polly is a UK-born occasion and going-out wear brand for women 18–30 who dress for the night, not the wardrobe — high-production, body-confident, built for TikTok, designed for the moment.

---

## 2. Audience

**Primary:** Women aged 18–30, US market
**Mindset:** Occasion-first shopper — they don't build wardrobes, they buy outfits for specific moments: birthday dinners, bachelorette weekends, club nights, holidays, weddings as a guest
**Platform:** TikTok and Instagram primary. Discovers brands through hauls, try-ons, and UGC before visiting the site
**Relationship with fashion:** Not a fashion purist — she wants to look incredible and feel desired, not to make a statement about taste
**Price sensitivity:** Mid-accessible — $80–150 feels aspirational but achievable. She stretches for the right dress.

---

## 3. Emotional Direction

**The site should make the visitor feel:**
- Desired
- Excited
- Confident

**The site must never feel:**
- Modest or conservative
- Clinical or corporate
- Generic or interchangeable with other DTC fashion brands

**Brand archetype:** Lover + Explorer
- The Lover: beauty, desire, intimacy, passion — dressing to be seen and felt
- The Explorer: she's going somewhere. Every outfit is a passport stamp.

---

## 4. Visual Identity

**Logo:** Elegant mixed-case wordmark — "oh" in script lowercase + "POLLY" in serif caps. Refined, feminine, confident. Not playful, not streetwear — occasion-ready.

**Photography style:**
- Studio-lit, high-production quality
- Black, cream, and neutral palette — let the garment color pop
- Body-confident framing — curve-forward, close-crop, editorial angles
- Models in motion or posed with intention — never stiff catalog poses
- Predominantly solo-model shots; some editorial multi-figure editorial

**Color palette (brand-supplied):**
- Near-black background for dark sections: #0a0a0a or #111
- Cream/warm white for light sections: #faf8f5
- Accent: deep rose or champagne gold — to be refined in design system
- Text: near-black (#111) on light, near-white (#faf8f5) on dark

**Reference brands (visual and tonal):**
- SKIMS — body confidence, studio-lit, restrained palette
- Jacquemus — editorial tension, desire, European coolness
- "SKIMS + Jacquemus had a daughter who grew up on TikTok" — this is the brief in one sentence

---

## 5. Site Type

**Type:** Brand landing page (not a headless e-commerce build)
**Commerce:** All shopping links route to the existing live Shopify store at us.ohpolly.com
**Primary action:** Browse New Arrivals (CTA links to us.ohpolly.com/collections/new-in)
**Secondary actions:** Explore collections, sign up to email list (Klaviyo)

---

## 6. Sections (Build Order)

1. **Announcement Bar** — "Free US shipping over $80 · Duties paid · Shop Now" — sticky, minimal, dismissible
2. **Navigation** — transparent → solid on scroll, centered logo, 5 links max, cart icon (links to store)
3. **Hero** — Full-viewport campaign video (autoplay, muted, loop) + headline + primary CTA
4. **New Arrivals** — 6–8 product cards pulling editorial lifestyle imagery, "Shop New In" CTA to store
5. **Editorial Collections** — 2–3 thematic collection blocks (e.g. The Wedding Edit, Going Out, Holiday), alternating image/copy layout
6. **Bestsellers** — Grid of 4–6 signature pieces, static (no Shopify API — use curated editorial images)
7. **Lookbook** — Full-bleed editorial image sequence, no text overlay, "RSVP: The Wedding Edit" campaign visual
8. **UGC Feed** — 6–8 customer/creator photos with @handles, "Worn by you" heading, links to Instagram
9. **Press / Stockist Strip** — As seen in logos or pull quotes (ASOS, Revolve, etc.)
10. **Email Capture** — Klaviyo signup, "Be first for new drops." — dark section, full-width, minimal
11. **Footer** — Links, US-specific signals (shipping, returns, sizing guide), social icons, legal

---

## 7. Copy Tone

**Voice:** Confident, direct, aspirational — never girlboss, never casual startup
**Sentence structure:** Short. Declarative. Present tense. Active.
**Vocabulary:** Occasion, moment, night, look, dress, campaign — not "pieces" not "styles" not "items"
**Do not use:** Exclamation marks in headlines, "Hey girl" energy, "obsessed", "slay" in body copy (fine in UGC captions), discount-first language

**Sample headlines (approved tone):**
- "Dressed for the moment you've been waiting for."
- "The Wedding Edit. Guest-approved."
- "New in. Worn tonight."
- "Built for your best nights."

---

## 8. Tech Constraints

**Email platform:** Klaviyo (mandatory — all email signup forms must POST to Klaviyo list)
**Shop links:** All "Shop" CTAs link to us.ohpolly.com — no embedded cart or product API
**Photography:** Real Oh Polly campaign and product photography — no placeholders, no stock imagery
**No lorem ipsum anywhere**

---

## 9. US Market Signals

- **Shipping:** Free US shipping on orders over $80 — in announcement bar and footer
- **Duties:** Duties paid (DDP) — in announcement bar and product sections
- **Returns:** US return policy link in footer
- **Sizing:** UK→US size conversion guide — critical, linked from nav and product sections
- **Currency:** USD only. Never GBP as default.
- **Social proof:** UGC from US creators preferred where available

---

## 10. Assets Confirmed Available

- Campaign hero video (looping, 15–30 seconds)
- 200–400 product lifestyle images
- "RSVP: The Wedding Edit" campaign imagery
- @ohpolly Instagram (1.8M followers) — UGC source
- Logo SVG (mixed-case wordmark)
- Press mentions (ASOS, Revolve, etc.)

---

## 11. What Success Looks Like

A US shopper who has never heard of Oh Polly lands on this page and within 10 seconds:
1. Understands this is a going-out/occasion wear brand
2. Feels the quality and desirability through the photography
3. Clicks "Shop New In" to the live store

The site is not trying to explain the brand. It is trying to make her want the dress.
